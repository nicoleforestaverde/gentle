Frequently asked questions

How do nudges work?

Tap + and answer two things: a day, and when in that day. The second answer is a preset, morning through night, plus anytime and all day. Anywhen draws a real minute inside that preset's window, keeps it inside your online hours, and rounds it to something tidy. Most reminder apps make you translate "this evening" into 19:00 before they will take it. Anywhen stores the answer you gave.

If you want an exact minute instead, pick Specific time and set the date and the time together. That one arrives to the minute and pierces Focus.

A preset nudge you have not dealt with tries again in each later part of the same day: something set for the morning speaks again in the afternoon, the evening and at night, then stops. It keeps doing that on the days it is scheduled for until you complete it, move it or delete it. A specific-time nudge does not do this. It arrives once, at its time.

What is Later, and what is Some other time?

Two ways to move a nudge, and you use one or the other.

Later is the quick one: it rolls the nudge to the next part of the day and commits straight away. The « and » arrows beside it step the preview further out without changing anything, so you can see where it would land before you take it.

Some other time opens a sentence you fill in: move this to a day, at a preset. Nothing moves until you tap OK. Tapping a day, a preset or Shuffle only changes what the sentence says; close the popup without confirming and the nudge keeps the time it had.

What does Someday do?

Someday is for things with no date that you still do not want to lose. A nudge shelved there speaks once, on a day drawn at random between 15 and 90 days from when you shelved it, at its own part of the day. It never repeats and never tries again.

The day is drawn per nudge, so a handful shelved in one sitting come back spread out rather than all on the same afternoon. It stays in your list the whole time, sorted by that date like everything else, so you can open it and give it a real day whenever you want. Shelving it again draws a new day.

Can a nudge repeat?

Yes, with Anywhen Plus. Open a nudge and tap Repeat: every day, every week, weekdays, weekends, every month, every year, or every N days with the number you type. Which cadences are offered depends on the day the nudge is set to.

Done this one completes the occurrence in front of you and the arrangement carries on. The trash on a repeat skips just that one occurrence. Finish series is the one that ends the whole thing.

If your Plus subscription lapses, a repeat is not deleted. It stops recurring and sits in your list holding its place, and the first time you complete an occurrence it parks: nothing fires, and the only thing left to do with it is delete it. Subscribe again and it picks up where it left off, with its cadence and its history intact.

Can I choose what time each part of the day means?

In Preferences, under "When each part lands", you can give morning, afternoon, evening and night a preferred time each. Nudges in that part land within about ten minutes of it, so several sharing one preference do not all arrive at the same minute. Leave a part on Surprise me and Anywhen draws a fresh time inside that window instead. The Shuffle button appears only for parts left on Surprise me, because a preferred time is an answer and re-rolling it would throw that answer away.

Under "Online hours" you can set the earliest and latest a nudge may land, and split weekdays from weekends if the two differ. Nothing lands outside those hours except a specific time, which is exactly what you asked for and is left alone.

I am not receiving notifications.

Check that notifications are allowed for Anywhen in Settings, then Notifications, then Anywhen. Anywhen uses local notifications, so it needs no connection and no account.

A nudge plays a sound by default. Switch it to Silent when you create it and it arrives quietly in Notification Center with a badge instead. A specific-time nudge comes through Focus; the others do not, so a Focus mode will hold them back.

If they arrive but at odd times, check your online hours in Preferences. A narrow window squeezes every preset into it.

Tapping a notification opens the app on that nudge with its card already open, or on Done if you have already completed it.

What is the free limit?

Three active nudges in total, not per day. Someday counts, and so does anything you share in from another app. The two examples Anywhen starts you with do not. Completing or deleting one frees a slot.

At the limit, sharing into Anywhen stops too: you get the paywall, and closing it without subscribing means the shared text is not kept. Anywhen Plus removes the limit and unlocks repeating nudges and all nineteen palettes.

Can I send things to Anywhen from other apps?

Yes. Share text or a link from any app and pick Anywhen, and it is filed to Someday so it does not interrupt anything. Anywhen takes quiet notification permission for this rather than asking you a system question mid-share, so shares deliver into Notification Center without ringing. Open the nudge afterwards to edit it or give it a real day.

How do I move my data to a new device?

In Preferences, under Your data, tap Back up. The file holds your nudges, your Done list and your settings.

Save it somewhere the new device can reach, such as Files or iCloud Drive, then open Preferences there and tap Restore. Anywhen cannot be opened straight from a shared file, so it has to be saved first. You will be asked to confirm before a restore replaces anything.

How do I cancel my Anywhen Plus subscription?

Preferences, then Manage subscription, which opens Apple's subscription screen. You can also get there through Settings, your name, Subscriptions. Deleting the app does not cancel it.

I reinstalled or changed device. Where is my subscription?

Open the paywall and tap Restore purchases. Anywhen Plus is tied to your App Store account, not to the device or the app data, so signing in with the same account gets it back.

Where is my data stored? Can you recover it?

Your nudges live on your device, plus wherever you choose to keep a backup file. Anywhen has no account and no server of ours, and your nudges are never sent anywhere. Each time you open Anywhen it asks RevenueCat, the service that tracks subscriptions, whether Anywhen Plus is active on your store account. That check carries an anonymous ID and your store receipt, and nothing else. If you subscribe, the payment itself goes through Apple. The Privacy Policy has the details.

Because we hold nothing, we cannot recover anything after the app is deleted or the device is reset, unless you saved a backup. A full device backup includes Anywhen.

How do I delete all my data?

Delete the app. That removes everything Anywhen stored on the device, though a backup file you saved earlier stays wherever you put it, and an Anywhen Plus subscription still needs cancelling separately.

Which languages are available?

English, German, French, Spanish, Portuguese, Korean and Japanese. Anywhen follows your device language when it is one of these, and you can also choose one in Preferences.
